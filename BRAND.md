# Sciovia — Brand Foundation

*This is the reference every edition, page, and post should follow. Keep it short and keep to it.*

---

## The name

**Sciovia** — coined from Latin *scio* ("I know") + *via* ("way"): **the path of knowing.**

- Always written **Sciovia** (capital S, rest lowercase). Never all-caps, never "SciOvia".
- Pronounced *see-OH-vee-ah*.
- Note for reference: "sciovia" is also an Italian word for a ski lift. This is harmless in the global research context and never mentioned in materials — but good to know if an Italian colleague smiles.

## Tagline

Primary: **The path of knowing.**

Alternates (use where a functional line fits better than the poetic one):
- *Helping researchers find their way.*
- *Direction for the research community.*

## Attribution — the one rule about the founder's name

Dr. Vinit Kumar Gunjan's name appears **only** in one of these two forms, always small and in a footer or byline — never in a headline, never with a photo-led "personal brand" framing:

- **An initiative by Dr. Vinit Kumar Gunjan**
- **Curated by Dr. Vinit Kumar Gunjan**

Sciovia is the subject. The founder is the quiet steward. Credibility is earned through consistent service, not visibility.

## What Sciovia is — and is not

| Sciovia is | Sciovia is not |
|---|---|
| Independent and unaffiliated | An official newsletter of any organization |
| A community service | A marketing or personal-branding channel |
| Curated and trustworthy | A firehose of unchecked links |

## Mission

To help researchers spend less time searching and more time doing meaningful work — by curating trustworthy opportunities and sharing practical, honest guidance.

## Vision

To become a research community that scholars voluntarily follow: a fair, reliable place to discover opportunities, celebrate good work, and uphold ethical research.

## Guiding principle

> Empowering researchers through knowledge sharing, collaboration, and opportunities.

## Values

1. **Service before self.** Usefulness to the reader decides everything.
2. **Accuracy over volume.** One verified item beats ten unchecked ones.
3. **Fairness.** Opportunities are shared on merit, not favor.
4. **Celebration.** We lift others up; the credit goes to them.
5. **Humility.** No hype, no exaggeration, no noise.

## Voice & writing rules

Sciovia should read as if written by a thoughtful senior colleague — clear enough for a first-year PhD, credible enough for a full professor.

**Do:**
- Be concise and specific. Lead with the fact (what, who, deadline).
- Use plain, internationally readable English.
- Be optimistic and encouraging without gushing.
- Attribute and link to the original source. Always.
- State deadlines as absolute dates (e.g. *15 August 2026*), never "next week".

**Don't:**
- No exaggeration ("world's best", "revolutionary", "must-not-miss").
- No unnecessary adjectives or filler.
- No AI-sounding scaffolding ("In today's fast-paced world…", "Let's dive in").
- No emoji clutter, no clickbait, no urgency tricks.
- Never claim, imply, or borrow an affiliation you don't have.

**A quick before/after:**

> ❌ *🚀 HUGE opportunity you absolutely cannot miss! This game-changing grant is revolutionizing research!*
>
> ✅ *Grant: The XYZ Foundation is accepting applications for early-career fellowships (up to $50,000). Deadline: 15 August 2026. [Details]*

## Visual identity

### The logo — a faceted compass rose

The mark is a four-point compass rose. Its meaning is the name itself: *scio* ("I know") + *via* ("the way") = **the path of knowing.**

- **The compass** — navigation and guidance. Sciovia helps researchers find their way.
- **The gold north point** — the guiding light: the opportunity worth reaching, and the warmth of service. (The other three points are pine green; only north is gold.)
- **The ring** — the global research community, and the horizon.
- **The center pivot** — the steady point everything turns around: trust.

**Assets** (in `assets/img/`):
| File | Use |
|---|---|
| `sciovia-mark.svg` | Symbol only — favicon, avatar, app icon |
| `sciovia-mark-light.svg` | Symbol for dark backgrounds |
| `sciovia-logo-horizontal.svg` | Mark + wordmark — website header, email, letterhead |
| `sciovia-logo-stacked.svg` | Mark above wordmark + tagline — social profile, posters |

Logo rules: keep clear space around it (at least the width of one compass point); never recolor, stretch, rotate, or add effects; never place the dark mark on a dark background (use the light variant).
