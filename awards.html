Excellence, honoured.

A distinguished programme celebrating the researchers who advance knowledge, mentor others, and serve the community — from the most promising newcomers to lifetimes of achievement.

The honours

Awards worthy of the work.

Each award recognises genuine merit, judged fairly by a panel of researchers. No fees, no favour — only excellence.

🏆

Researcher of the Year

Our flagship honour — for a researcher whose work over the year stood out for its quality, originality, and contribution to the field.

🌟

Emerging Researcher Award

Celebrating an outstanding early-career researcher (within seven years of the PhD) of exceptional promise.

🎖️

Lifetime Achievement Award

Honouring a distinguished researcher for a sustained body of work and an enduring contribution to knowledge.

📜

Research Excellence Award

For an outstanding publication or research contribution of exceptional rigour and significance.

🌍

Research Impact Award

Recognising research that has made a tangible difference to society, industry, policy, or the wider world.

🤝

Distinguished Mentor Award

For a mentor whose guidance has shaped and elevated the next generation of researchers.

The programme also honours Women in Research and Research Integrity & Peer Review, with new categories introduced as the community grows.

Every month · alongside the annual honours

Awards of the Month

Two monthly awards celebrating excellence in teaching and research — evidence-based, merit-only, and given only when genuinely earned.

🔬

Research Award of the Month

For a notable, verifiable research contribution — a peer-reviewed paper, dataset, or patent from that month — judged on quality, rigour, and significance. The work must be linked.

🎓

Teaching Award of the Month

For a documented teaching contribution — an open course or resource, a pedagogical innovation, or outstanding mentorship — supported by clear evidence.

How we keep them genuine

✔  Evidence required — every recipient is tied to verifiable work, with a link.

✔  Reviewed by a panel against published criteria — no fee, no favour.

✔  One recipient per award each month, at most.

✔  Skipped when unearned — if no one clearly deserves it, we don't give it.

Recipients receive a certificate of recognition and a featured citation in the Sciovia newsletter and honours archive.

How it works

Fair, transparent, merit-only.

1

Nominate

Anyone may nominate a researcher — or themselves. A short, honest case for the work is all it takes.

2

Review

A panel of researchers evaluates each nomination on merit alone — no fees, no favour, no self-promotion.

3

Celebrate

Recipients are announced and honoured at the annual Sciovia Awards, and featured across the community.
