Running Sciovia — a simple guide

Everything the managing committee needs to run Sciovia day to day: approving members, replying to people, handling submissions and nominations, and sending the newsletter. No technical knowledge needed — it's all done in one Google Sheet.

🧭 The one place you work: the Sciovia Members sheet

Almost everything you do happens in a single Google Sheet called “Sciovia Members.” It has a special Sciovia menu at the top and these tabs at the bottom:

TabWhat it holds
ApplicationsPeople applying for membership — you approve or decline here.
MessagesMessages sent through the website's contact form.
SubmissionsOpportunities the public submitted via "List an event."
NominationsAward nominations sent by members.
SubscribersPeople who signed up for the weekly newsletter.
NewsletterWhere you write each issue's intro and tips.

Your actions all live in the Sciovia menu at the top of the sheet.

💡 A second sheet, “Sciovia Opportunities,” is where your five section editors post listings. You don't need to touch it day to day — it runs itself. (Their guide: editor-guide.)

🪪 Approving (or declining) members

Your main recurring task. Open the Applications tab.

The Applications tab — set each row's Status to Approved or Rejected, then run the menu.

1
Read the application.

Each row has the person's name, email, requested tier, field, and a profile link. Check they're a genuine researcher.

2
Look at "Referred by".

If this column shows a member's Sciovia ID (or says "member nomination"), a current member has vouched for them — you can fast-track and approve with a light check.

3
Decide, in the "Status" column:

Type Approved to accept — or Rejected to decline (and, if you like, write a short reason in the Note column).

4
Run it.

Menu: Sciovia → Process applications (approve / decline).

✅ Approved people automatically get a welcome email with their Member Pass and a Sciovia ID (SCV-2026-000001, 000002…). Declined people get a polite email with your reason. You don't send anything by hand.

✉️ Replying to messages

Open the Messages tab.

Every contact-form message is saved here and emailed to teamsciovia@gmail.com. To reply, just answer the email in your inbox as normal — the person's address is on it. Nothing else to do.

📥 Opportunities the public sends in

Open the Submissions tab.

1
Review each submission for accuracy — is it real, with a working link?
2
If it's good, pass it to the right section editor, or add it yourself to the matching tab in the Sciovia Opportunities sheet and tick Published.
3
If not, just leave it. Nothing goes public automatically.

🏆 Award nominations

Open the Nominations tab.

Members submit award nominations through the website (they must give their membership number). Each nomination lists the award, the nominee, the reason, and who nominated them. The awards panel reviews these on merit when each award cycle comes around.

📧 Sending the weekly newsletter

People who subscribed are in the Subscribers tab. To send them a digest:

1
Menu: Sciovia → Newsletter → Set up newsletter tab (first time only).
2
In the Newsletter tab, write the Issue label, a short Intro, a Researcher of the Week, and a Practical Tip. (The opportunities are added automatically — you don't type those.)
3
Menu: Sciovia → Newsletter → Send test to me → check it in your inbox.
4
When it looks right: Sciovia → Newsletter → Send to all subscribers.

The Newsletter tab — you fill the Intro, Researcher, and Tip; the opportunities are added automatically.

📌 Free Gmail sends about 100 emails a day — plenty to start. When the list grows past that, we'll move sending to a bulk tool (Substack/Beehiiv).

🌟 The golden rules

  • Accuracy over volume. Only publish or approve things you've checked. One verified item beats ten unchecked ones.
  • Be warm and humble. Sciovia serves the community — no hype, no exaggeration, no favouritism.
  • No personal names in public. Everything is presented as the work of the Sciovia Managing Committee, not any individual.
  • Keep it fresh. Remove opportunities once their deadline passes, so the list stays trustworthy.
  • Reply kindly. Even a decline should leave someone feeling respected.

❓ Need help?

Email hello@sciovia.org, or check the technical setup notes in the project's backend/SETUP.md. If a menu item doesn't appear, reload the sheet (F5) — the Sciovia menu builds itself each time the sheet opens.
